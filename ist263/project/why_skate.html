Why trust my advice?

A fair question, but I've been skating for a good while now.

When I first started skating, I paid $200 for a very basic 38-inch Sector 9 board. It’s a lovely board, but it was too much for what I got. Now I know what I'm doing, and have spent considerably less on all of my five boards.

My first custom deck

After having skated for close to 6 years now, I’m still not very good. I can do one slide with gloves, can confidently get myself from A to B, and can manage hills OK. I skate almost every day during the school season and typically only take a spill a couple of times a year. But I’m happy with where I am. Unlike a lot of sports, its something you can take at your own pace and comfort level. There’s never anyone egging you to go beyond your limits or to do something dangerous. Skating gives you a similar level of freedom to biking without the encumbering size, to roller blading/skating without the time-consuming lacing, to roller skiing without looking like a total nerd. It’s a far more pleasant way to get around than using a car, and there’s no better feeling than having the freedom to get anywhere in a mile radius in just a couple of minutes without having to start a combustion engine.

New Ideas

I tried skateboarding when I was little, but didn’t have the patience. I thought longboarding was really dumb when I first heard about it in high school and teased my friends that it was just skateboarding (which, it is, but it's also different) until I stopped being annoying and gave it a shot after a year. In 10th grade my friend lent me his sturdy Arbor board for a couple of weeks. I didn’t even have a skate tool to adjust how tight the trucks were, but every day after school I still walked down my hill and practiced skating Longboarding gave me a reason to spend afternoons outside again.

Eventually I knew I had to get a board for myself, so I made the trek with my mom to the closest Zumiez. In that mall an hour away I overpaid for a Sector Nine double-kick drop-through board. I didn't get my second board until I went away to colleg. I got my third board courtesy of a non-skater friend who won a contest at the end of my freshman year. My fourth board was a large cruiser I got my first semester in Syracuse. I bought it at the very end of fall, so I had to wait an entire winter before I really got to use it.

Recently I purchased a real honest to god skateboard which desperately needs new bearings. I haven’t had time to learn to ride it—I really didn’t expect how different it would feel though. It’s almost like starting from scratch.
