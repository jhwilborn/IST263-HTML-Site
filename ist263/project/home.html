What is Skating?

Interested in trying longboarding or skateboarding? You’ve come to the right place. On this site I’ll give you a crash course in skate terminology, a course in how to avoid crashing (and getting hurt), and why I love skating so much that I do it every day! Why can you trust me? I’ve been skating for 6 years and since I came to Syracuse, it’s been my primary mode of travel to get where I need to go daily. I have 5 boards that I love dearly and do my best to use and take care of given my busy schedule, and I’m happy to teach anyone who’s interested to skate. Frankly I’m a huge fan of anything with wheels that doesn't have a combustion engine.


What's the difference? Longboard versus Skateboard

All boards exist on a continuity between penny board and long board. The smallest board is the penny or mini-cruiser, the former is less than 20 inches long and the latter less than 26 inches long. Penny Board is a brand name, and they make plastic boards that are the smallest a person can reasonably stand on. Mini cruisers can be made of any material, but they are slightly longer and wider than penny boards.

The next size up is the skateboard, which is notable for its design rather than its size. Unlike many longboards, a skateboard is symmetrical on both sides and comes in many different lengths, although the typical size is 28 inches. Next to skateboards stand cruiser boards, which are often asymmetrical and have bigger, softer wheels than skateboards, even though the deck may be the same size. At the top end, from 37 inches and onward, are the longboards. There are no limits on how long these are, and some may be as much as 60 inches in length.

Quick terms and techniques

Technique

  • Carving – Making wide turns across a roadway to maintain a low speed and high degree of control
  • Switch – Riding with your primary foot on the back of the board.
  • Sliding – Deliberately breaking traction to quickly reduce speed or make tight turns
  • Freestyle – Technical longboarding riding style which utilizes sliding while standing, tight trucks, and elements of skateboarding to perform tricks on the road
  • Downhill – Rapid descent of steep hills, typically performed with a full-face helmet and a significant amount of padding. The most popular genre of professional longboarding.
  • Cruising – Casual riding on flat or slightly sloped roads. Low speed, low impact, and the safest way to skate.
  • Dancing – Performed on extra long boards on flat ground. Involves low speed tricks and full body rotations and jumps on a moving board.
  • Regular or Goofy stance – Regular rides left foot forward. Goofy rides right foot forward.

Terminology

  • Bail – crash
  • Ate it/eat it – crash, also
  • Bombing – to go down a hill really fast
  • Wheel bite – When wheels fitted underneath a board make contact with the deck. This can cause a wheel to slow down or stop, throwing the rider from the board.
  • Pushing/pumping – Using your back foot to gain speed on a board by planting it on the ground while your front foot stays on the deck.
  • Mongo pushing – Pushing with your front foot. Considered limiting by many experienced riders.
